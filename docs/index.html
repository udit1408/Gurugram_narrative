Plain-Language Reading

  • The corrected hydraulic run does not allow flood cells inside building footprints. Buildings receive no direct rainfall; roof runoff is redistributed to nearby active non-building surface.
  • Absolute depth maps use a fixed discrete hazard scale from 0.05 m to 15 m. Difference maps use green for reduction and red for local increase or transferred water.
  • The persistent deep cell remains a survey trigger near road/subgrade context. It is no longer a building-footprint artifact, but it still needs field levels before being treated as ordinary road depth.
  • The critical subgrade identifier is SUB-01: Golf Course Road Underpass / Rapid MetroRail Gurgaon context. It is mapped separately from ordinary road waterlogging.
  • Pump egress is screened to sumps, parks/public grounds, or open/fallow low receptors. Open-land receptors remain field-check required for groundwater depth, water quality, utilities, and permission.

What Is Included

  • Corrected choked no-pump, partial drainage, choked-plus-pumps, partial-drainage-plus-pumps, pre-filled drainage/sump no-pump, and pre-filled drainage/sump plus pumps scenarios.
  • Layer-controlled map for depth, velocity, persistence, buildings, roads, drainage, egress, recharge candidates, official waterlogging points, subgrade survey trigger, flow directions, and adaptive refinement zones.
  • Side-by-side scenario map, pump-difference map, drainage-difference map, time series, and lat/lon pump table.

How To Use Each Tab

Suggested flow: start here, then use Layer Map or Ward Map for screening, Query for a specific point, and finish with Evidence, Backflow, Pump Table, and Method & QA before action.

Overview
Read the screening story first.

Use this tab for the plain-language interpretation, the list of included scenarios, and the key limits on what decisions the dashboard can and cannot support.

Layer Map
Screen one scenario on the main operational map.

Pick a scenario and flood variable, toggle drainage and field layers, and draw an AOI box when you need area-level statistics instead of one-point results.

Impact Atlas
Compare scenario outputs side by side.

Use this when you want a fast visual comparison of how depth, velocity, or persistence shifts between scenarios without changing map controls one by one.

Pump Effect
Measure what pumps and drainage change.

This tab contains pump-on and drainage-difference views, plus time-series summaries that help show whether improvements are local, transferred, or persistent.

Animation
See when flooding arrives, peaks, and recedes.

Use the time animation and progression maps to understand timing, not just final-state depth, especially for response sequencing and pump deployment windows.

Ward Map
Zoom into one ward with hover readouts.

Crop to Wards 21 to 24, then hover to read local depth, velocity, and persistence for the selected scenario and operational layer combination.

Query
Check one location, pump ID, egress ID, or coordinate.

Enter decimal coordinates, DMS, a named place, or an asset ID to drop a marker, snap to the nearest flooded active cell when needed, and read local scenario statistics.

Evidence
Cross-check uploaded field evidence with the model.

Use this tab to review the waterlogging register, matched field locations, and where the model agrees or disagrees with reported observations.

Backflow
Review ranked inlet and manhole surcharge points.

This table highlights where the coupled drainage model sends water back to the surface so field teams can prioritize drain checks and confirm the physical asset nearby.

Pump Table
Review pump locations, egress choices, and actuation.

Use this for implementation detail: where pumps sit, which screened egress destination each one uses, and how the fleet behaves by scenario.

Method & QA
Check assumptions, QA, and caveats before sharing.

Open this tab when you need the technical notes, validation logic, data caveats, and the safest wording for external communication or review.

Use Limitations

This dashboard is a decision-support screening product, not a certified engineering design, statutory flood map, or final emergency order by itself. Field verification is required before pump deployment, civil works, recharge approval, or public closure decisions.

Critical assumptions include available terrain products, mapped drainage topology, assumed 20 HP pump field capacity, temporary virtual downstream relief points in drainage-working cases, and the subgrade/underpass survey trigger at SUB-01.

Impact Atlas

This replaces the difficult six-map visual comparison. Panel 1 gives the baseline choked-drainage hazard. Panels 2-4 show intervention effect directly: green means the intervention lowered final depth, red means the intervention raised local final depth, and gray means change below 3 cm is hidden.

Red with pumps does not mean pumps create water. The pump operator conserves mass: water removed from intake cells is added at screened egress/receptor cells. Red areas therefore mark receiving pockets, local backwater, or places where pumped water is intentionally relocated and must be managed.

Pump-On Difference

Green means reduced final depth relative to choked no-pump. Red means local increase where water has been moved or retained at egress areas. This is expected in a mass-conserving pump model: pumped water is relocated, not deleted.

Drainage Difference

Green means the partial-drainage case reduced final depth relative to the choked case. Red pockets require local explanation before operational interpretation.

Partial Drainage + Pumps

Green means the pump-enabled partial-drainage case reduced final depth relative to partial drainage without pumps. Red means transferred water, local retention near the egress receptor, or backwater where the receiving area is also wet.

Method And QA Notes

  • The AIResQ physics-AI coupled flood resolver uses building enforcement: building centroids are obstruction-elevated, receive zero direct rainfall, and are excluded from active-surface reporting.
  • Outer boundary is transmissive/open. Boundary flux closure is reported separately because water can legitimately leave or enter at the domain edge.
  • Rainfall is Horton-excess rainfall derived from prepared LULC/infiltration rasters; roof rainfall is redistributed to nearby non-building receiver cells.
  • Drainage is represented through mapped MCG/NAS collector topology, inlets, local storage, surcharge-return rules, and three temporary virtual downstream relief points for the drainage-working cases. The virtual relief points prevent water from remaining artificially trapped after rainfall stops.
  • Pump scenario is mass-conserving: volume removed from intake cells is added back at screened egress cells, so local increases near receptors are expected.
  • Pre-filled drainage/sump scenarios start mapped network storage at 90% full before rainfall. These are stress tests for blocked downstream or already-loaded drainage conditions.
  • Pump sites were regenerated after building enforcement using the corrected choked and partial-drainage outputs.
  • Park/open/fallow receptor use is not approval for recharge. Seasonal water table, infiltration, water quality, utilities, access, ownership, and permission must be checked.
  • The remaining high-depth subgrade/road pocket is carried as a survey trigger and should be verified with local DTM/road crown/ramp/invert data.

Data, Accuracy, And Use Caveats

  • Purpose: screening-level decision support for prioritization, field checks, pump staging, and scenario comparison. It should not be used alone as a certified drainage design, legal flood boundary, land acquisition basis, or public safety order.
  • Terrain: model accuracy is limited by available DSM/DTM/DEM quality. Patchy or over-smoothed terrain, road crowns, underpasses, basement ramps, curbs, and local inverts can materially change hyperlocal depths.
  • SUB-01: the extreme-depth location is treated as an underpass/subgrade survey trigger. Confirm invert levels, sump condition, tailwater, and road-ramp connectivity before interpreting it as ordinary surface-water depth.
  • Drainage: mapped MCG/NAS drainage geometry, inlets, sumps, and collector continuity are used where available. Drainage-working cases include temporary virtual downstream relief points where downstream continuity is plausible but field-verified outfall geometry is pending.
  • Pumps: pump locations and capacities are planning placeholders based on an assumed 20 HP municipal mobile-pump class and field capacity. Actual pump curves, hose length, static head, fuel/power, crew access, and safe discharge permission must be verified.
  • Recharge or egress: parks/open lands are only screened receptor candidates. Water table, soil infiltration, water quality, ownership, utilities, ecological sensitivity, and public permission must be checked before use.
  • Map display: smoothed map and animation surfaces improve readability. Quantitative values should be read from the hover tooltip, tables, and exported model data. A smoothed red footprint may be wider than a narrow peak-depth model cell.
  • Calibration: outputs should be updated after field survey, observed water-level traces, verified drainage operation, and actual pump performance records become available.
